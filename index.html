Straight answers to what Caribbean business owners actually ask before they engage us.

What if something breaks? Who supports it?
+

Every deployment we ship includes monitoring, alerting, and a dedicated operational contact on our Managed Automation retainer. If something breaks, we know before you do — our systems alert us in real time. For clients on a one-time build without a retainer, we provide a 30-day hypercare period post-launch at no charge, covering bug fixes and configuration adjustments. After that, retainer packages start at TT$800/mo. We don't ship and disappear. Caribbean businesses can't afford systems that go dark when something shifts — and neither can we afford the reputation hit of that happening.

Included in all Managed Automation Plans
Is this suitable for my size of business?
+

Yes — and we've built specifically for both ends of the market. MSME operators (a single storefront, a service business, a small logistics company) are often our fastest wins. You don't need a large operation to benefit from automation — you just need a process that's eating time or leaking revenue. Our smallest MSME clients start with a single WhatsApp automation or payments integration. The ROI is typically visible within the first two weeks. Enterprise clients (energy, logistics, healthcare) engage us for multi-department builds with compliance and governance layers. The free discovery call exists exactly for this question — we'll tell you honestly whether automation makes sense for your operation right now, and what the realistic entry point looks like.

MSME operators typically start at TT$17k–$35k
Do I need to change my existing software or systems?
+

Almost never. We build the automation layer on top of what you already use — WhatsApp, your accounting software, your existing spreadsheets if necessary. Our job is to connect and orchestrate your existing tools, not replace them. In cases where a tool is genuinely creating bottlenecks (for example, a legacy system with no API), we'll flag it in the discovery call and recommend the lowest-friction path forward. But in the vast majority of engagements, your team keeps using the tools they know — they just stop doing the manual work those tools used to require. The transition period is typically 2–4 weeks for a pilot, during which your operation runs in parallel with the new system until you're confident.

We integrate with your stack, not around it
How long does it actually take?
+

The discovery call is 30 minutes. A Diagnostics + Pilot is typically 2–4 weeks from kick-off to a working workflow in production. A Full Implementation runs 4–12 weeks depending on scope and the number of integrations involved. We operate on a 30-day KPI delivery model — meaning you see measurable results within the first 30 days of deployment, not months later. That's a deliberate design choice, not a marketing claim. We scope every engagement to hit a visible win quickly, then build from there. The slowest part of any engagement is usually the client-side: getting access to existing systems, getting internal sign-off, and aligning on the first workflow to automate. If you come to the discovery call knowing your biggest bottleneck, we can have a pilot running in under two weeks.

Pilot in 2–4 weeks · Full build in 4–12 weeks

Can I start with one workflow and add more later?
+

That's exactly how we recommend you start. 85% of our MSME clients begin with a single-workflow pilot — payments, WhatsApp commerce, or lead automation — then expand once they see it working. The pilot exists to prove value before you commit to full-scale deployment. You get one workflow automated, KPI targets at 30 days, and a clear roadmap for what to fix next. If the numbers justify it, you scale. If they don't, you walk away with one working automation and no regrets. Most clients who see positive ROI on the pilot add 2–3 additional workflows within the first 12 months. But there's zero obligation. The pilot is designed to stand alone.

Start with one workflow · Scale when ready
Do you work with legacy systems? We're not running the newest tech.
+

We build on top of what you already have. WhatsApp Business. Spreadsheets. Email threads. Legacy accounting software with no API. TSTT infrastructure. Cash-heavy operations. Carnival band management running out of a notebook. If it's running your business right now — even inefficiently — we can probably automate around it. The only time we recommend replacing a tool is when it's genuinely creating a bottleneck that costs more to work around than to fix. In most cases, your team keeps using the tools they know — they just stop doing the manual work those tools used to require. We wire the automation layer on top and orchestrate what's already there.

We integrate with your stack, not around it
What happens to our data? Is this secure?
+

Your data stays yours. We don't sell it, share it, or store it longer than operationally necessary. Every deployment includes encrypted backups, audit logs, and role-based access control. For enterprise clients (energy, healthcare, logistics), we meet compliance requirements for your sector — GDPR-aligned data handling, confidentiality standards, governance controls. If you're subject to regulatory oversight, we design the system to pass audit. For MSME operators, we explain what data is being captured, where it's stored, and who can access it — in plain language, before deployment. No surprise data exposure six months later.

Encrypted · Audit-ready · Compliance-aligned
What if my operation changes? Can workflows be updated?
+

Yes — and they should be. Businesses evolve. Workflows that made sense at 10 employees don't make sense at 50. A system that can't adapt is just another thing to replace later. Clients on our Managed Automation retainer get continuous optimization sprints — we adjust workflows as your operation changes, add new integrations as your stack grows, and run improvement cycles based on KPI performance. For clients who did a one-time build without a retainer, workflow updates are billed as change requests. Most MSME operators do 1–2 updates per year as they scale or add services. The system you deploy today should still be serving you two years from now — not gathering dust.
